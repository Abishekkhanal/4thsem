Entrepreneur

Concepts of Entrepreneur

Entrepreneur: An individual who creates, organizes, and operates a business, taking on financial risks to do so.

Definitions: Various definitions highlight different aspects such as innovation, risk-taking, and economic contribution.

Characteristics: Key traits include creativity, resilience, leadership, risk-taking, and problem-solving skills.

Classification: Entrepreneurs can be classified based on different criteria such as type of business (e.g., commercial, social), size (small, medium, large), and innovation level (innovative, imitative).

Entrepreneurial Traits

Specific qualities such as motivation, vision, initiative, persistence, and the ability to adapt to change.

Entrepreneurial Functions

Activities such as identifying opportunities, mobilizing resources, risk management, decision-making, and innovation.

Role in Economic Development

Entrepreneurs drive economic growth by creating jobs, fostering innovation, increasing productivity, and improving living standards.

Factors Affecting Growth

Includes access to finance, market conditions, government policies, technological advancements, and socio-cultural factors.

Entrepreneurship

Meaning: The process of starting and running a new business venture to achieve profits and growth.

Definition: Entrepreneurship is the act of creating, managing, and scaling a business to achieve economic and social value.

Entrepreneur vs Intrapreneur

Entrepreneurs start and run their own businesses, while intrapreneurs innovate within existing organizations.

Women Entrepreneurs

Female entrepreneurs who create and run businesses, often facing unique challenges such as gender bias and balancing family responsibilities.

Recent Developments

Trends include the rise of tech startups, increased support for women entrepreneurs, focus on sustainability, and the impact of digital transformation.

Problems

Challenges include access to funding, regulatory hurdles, market competition, and lack of skilled workforce.

Entrepreneurial Development Programmes (EDP)

Objectives: EDPs aim to develop entrepreneurial skills, foster innovation, and support new business ventures.

Methods of Training: Includes workshops, mentorship, hands-on projects, and business simulations.

Phases of EDP: Typically involves pre-training (selection of participants), training (skill development), and post-training (support and monitoring).

Institutional Support and Incentives

Department of Industries and Commerce (DIC)

Functions: Provides support and resources to businesses, including registration and guidance.

Small Industrial Development Corporation (SIDCO)

Activities: Supports small industries through infrastructure and development projects.

National Small Industries Corporation (NSIC)

Functions: Offers marketing support, financial aid, and technology services to small businesses.

Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI)

Functions: Provides financial assistance and support services to MSMEs.

Khadi Village Industry Commission (KVIC)

Functions: Promotes traditional village industries through training and financial support.

Small Industries Service Institute (SISI)

Functions and Services: Offers technical consultancy and support to small industries.

Kerala Industrial Technical Consultancy Organisation (KITCO)

Functions and Services: Provides project consultancy and technical services to businesses.

Science and Technology Entrepreneurship Development Project (STEDP)

Activities: Encourages science and technology-based entrepreneurship.

National Entrepreneurship Development Board (NEDB)

Strategies: Formulates policies and strategies to promote entrepreneurship.

National Institute for Entrepreneurship and Small Business Development (NIESBUD)

Objectives: Develops training programs and research to support small businesses.

Techno Park

Functions: Provides infrastructure and support for technology startups.

Incentives: Offers tax benefits, subsidies, and grants.

Subsidy: Financial assistance to reduce costs for startups.

MSME Act 2006

Salient Features: Provides a legal framework for the classification and promotion of MSMEs, simplifies the process of setting up and running MSMEs, offers various incentives and support mechanisms.

Credit Guarantee Fund Trust Scheme for MSMEs

Offers collateral-free credit to micro and small enterprises, encouraging banks to lend to MSMEs by providing a guarantee cover.

Industrial Estates

Classification: Categorized into large, medium, and small estates based on the size and number of industries accommodated.

Benefits: Provides infrastructure, reduces setup costs, and promotes industrial development in a planned manner.

Financial Assistance

Green Channel: A fast-track mechanism for quick loan processing for MSMEs.

Bridge Capital: Short-term financial assistance to bridge the gap between project implementation and revenue generation.

Seed Capital Assistance: Initial funding to support the startup phase of MSMEs.

Margin Money Schemes: Provides a portion of the project cost to reduce the burden on entrepreneurs.

Single Window System: A one-stop solution for obtaining various approvals and clearances required for setting up MSMEs.

Sickness in SSI

Causes: Includes inadequate working capital, poor management, obsolete technology, and market competition.

Remedies: Involves restructuring loans, providing technical support, improving management practices, and modernizing technology.

Registration of SSI

Involves registering with the relevant state or central authorities, obtaining a provisional registration certificate, and fulfilling necessary requirements for permanent registration.

Setting Up of Industrial Unit

Environment for Entrepreneurship

Creating a conducive environment includes access to funding, supportive regulatory frameworks, availability of skilled labor, and infrastructure.

Criteria for Selecting a Particular Project

Factors such as market demand, profitability, feasibility, technological feasibility, and alignment with personal skills and interests.

Generating Project Ideas

Methods include brainstorming, market research, identifying personal passions and problems, and observing trends.

Market and Demand Analysis

Assessing market size, potential growth, competition, customer preferences, and trends to determine demand for the product or service.

Feasibility Study

Technical Feasibility: Assessing whether the project can be developed using current technology and resources.

Financial Feasibility: Determining whether the project is financially viable, considering costs, revenue projections, and return on investment.

Social Cost-Benefit Analysis

Evaluating the project's impact on society, including benefits such as job creation and economic growth, and costs such as environmental impact.

Government Regulations for Project Clearance

Includes obtaining permits, licenses, environmental clearances, and approvals from regulatory authorities before starting operations.

Import of Capital Goods

Process involves obtaining an Importer Exporter Code (IEC), complying with customs regulations, and paying applicable duties and taxes.

Approval of Foreign Collaboration

Requires approval from the Foreign Investment Promotion Board (FIPB) or the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) for foreign direct investment (FDI) and technology transfer.

Pollution Control Clearances

Obtaining approvals from pollution control boards for managing and mitigating environmental impact through compliance with pollution control norms.

Setting Up of Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises

Location Decision: Factors include proximity to raw materials, market accessibility, infrastructure availability, and labor supply.

Significance: Choosing the right location impacts operational costs, market reach, and overall business success.
